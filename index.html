The Better Basics Approach.

_____


"Look, listen and learn"


Every business is different, because everybody is different; the way things are done, the way we communicate, what we sell and how we sell it. This is a key differentiator to the Better Basics approach – specific guidance based on understanding each individual business we work with.

Depending on the services required, we are flexible in our approach, but fundamentally we want to work through the challenges faced with those individuals that need to affect the change, empowering them to understand what to do, how to do it and why it needs to be done. This is done on an incremental basis to drive effective results.

There are four main pillars that drive performance:

1. People.

The most important part. People should be your biggest asset but often if mismanaged or misdirected, can be your biggest liability. Key areas to look at are structure, culture, communications and performance management.

2. Products.

What you sell, why people buy it, who else is selling it (or something similar) and how it could be improved are key areas to drive high performance of the business.

3. Processes.

Fundamental to making the business as efficient as possible as well as ensuring quality and legal criteria are consistently met and monitored. This includes the methods we use to do things and how they are documented so people can remember and refer back to them.

4. Performance.

To really succeed, it is essential a business moves from making decisions on a “feels like” basis to a data driven, evidence basis to ensure everyone is making more of the right choices. Measure what matters!
